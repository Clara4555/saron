Our Role in the Energy Transition

At Saron Inc., we recognize that the global energy landscape is evolving rapidly toward a more sustainable future. As a leading storage infrastructure provider, we are uniquely positioned to play a critical role in supporting and enabling this energy transition.

We are actively adapting our facilities and services to accommodate the changing energy mix, from traditional fossil fuels to renewable and low-carbon alternatives. Our commitment to the energy transition is reflected in strategic investments in infrastructure, technology, and expertise that can support both traditional and emerging energy markets.

By leveraging our global terminal network and deep industry experience, we are helping our clients navigate the complexities of the energy transition while ensuring reliable, safe, and environmentally responsible storage solutions for the full spectrum of energy products.

Our Energy Transition Strategy

  • Infrastructure Adaptation: Modifying existing facilities and developing new infrastructure to handle renewable and low-carbon fuels
  • Product Diversification: Expanding our product portfolio to include biofuels, hydrogen, ammonia, and other alternative energy carriers
  • Carbon Reduction: Implementing technologies and practices to reduce the carbon footprint of our operations
  • Innovation: Investing in research and pilot projects to develop solutions for emerging energy products
  • Partnerships: Collaborating with industry leaders, technology providers, and research institutions to accelerate the energy transition

"The energy transition represents both a challenge and an opportunity for the storage industry. At Saron, we are committed to being at the forefront of this transformation, providing the infrastructure and services needed to support a more sustainable energy future."

Chief Strategy Officer, Saron Inc.

Key Energy Transition Projects

Our flagship initiatives supporting the shift to cleaner energy sources.

Rotterdam Biofuels Hub

Renewable Fuels Infrastructure

Our Rotterdam terminal is being transformed into a major hub for biofuels and renewable fuels. This project includes the conversion of existing tanks for biofuel storage, installation of specialized handling equipment, and development of blending capabilities to support the increasing demand for sustainable transportation fuels.

  • Capacity: 500,000 barrels of dedicated biofuels storage
  • Products: Biodiesel, ethanol, SAF (Sustainable Aviation Fuel), HVO (Hydrotreated Vegetable Oil)
  • Features: Temperature-controlled storage, specialized blending systems, dedicated marine and truck loading facilities
  • Status: Phase 1 Complete (2023), Phase 2 In Progress

Houston Hydrogen Project

Future Fuels Development

In partnership with leading energy companies, we are developing hydrogen storage capabilities at our Houston terminal. This pioneering project will provide the infrastructure needed to support the emerging hydrogen economy, focusing initially on industrial applications with future expansion to support transportation fuels.

  • Scope: Development of specialized storage systems for hydrogen, including pressurized and liquid hydrogen options
  • Applications: Industrial hydrogen supply, refueling infrastructure support, power generation
  • Partners: Collaboration with technology providers, hydrogen producers, and end users
  • Status: Pilot Phase (2023-2024)

Singapore Carbon Reduction Initiative

Operational Decarbonization

Our Singapore terminal serves as a showcase for our comprehensive approach to reducing operational carbon emissions. This multi-faceted project includes renewable energy integration, energy efficiency improvements, and innovative carbon capture technologies, positioning the facility as a model for sustainable terminal operations.

  • Initiatives: 5MW solar installation, electrification of equipment, energy management systems, advanced emissions monitoring
  • Targets: 50% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 2030 (vs. 2020 baseline)
  • Recognition: Recipient of Singapore's Green Mark Platinum certification
  • Status: Implementation Phase (2022-2025)

Ammonia Ready Terminal - Corpus Christi

Alternative Energy Carrier

Our Corpus Christi expansion includes infrastructure designed to be "ammonia ready," anticipating the growing role of ammonia as a carrier for hydrogen and as a low-carbon fuel. This forward-looking project prepares us to support the emerging ammonia value chain while maintaining flexibility for traditional products.

  • Design: Purpose-built tanks and handling systems capable of storing refrigerated ammonia
  • Applications: Support for ammonia as a hydrogen carrier, marine fuel, and industrial feedstock
  • Safety: Advanced safety systems and specialized training for ammonia handling
  • Status: Design Phase (Construction 2024-2026)

New Energy Products

Expanding our capabilities to support the evolving energy landscape.

Biofuels

Comprehensive storage solutions for various biofuels, including biodiesel, ethanol, renewable diesel, and sustainable aviation fuel (SAF). Our facilities incorporate specialized systems for temperature control, product segregation, and quality maintenance to preserve the integrity of these sensitive products.

Hydrogen

Developing storage capabilities for hydrogen in various forms, including high-pressure gaseous storage, liquid hydrogen, and chemical carriers. Our hydrogen projects focus on creating the infrastructure needed to support the hydrogen economy, from production and distribution to end-use applications.

Ammonia

Specialized facilities for the storage and handling of ammonia, which is emerging as an important carrier for hydrogen and a potential low-carbon fuel for shipping. Our ammonia-ready terminals incorporate the extensive safety systems and specialized equipment required for this product.

Battery Materials

Storage solutions for the raw materials and precursors used in battery production, supporting the growing electric vehicle and energy storage markets. These facilities feature climate-controlled environments, advanced fire protection systems, and specialized handling equipment.

Circular Economy Products

Infrastructure to support circular economy initiatives, including storage for recycled oils, chemical feedstocks derived from plastic waste, and other recovered materials. These facilities help close the loop on material flows and reduce reliance on virgin resources.

e-Fuels & Synthetic Fuels

Preparing for the emergence of electrically-produced synthetic fuels that combine hydrogen with captured carbon dioxide to create drop-in replacements for conventional fuels. Our infrastructure adaptations anticipate the specific requirements of these future energy carriers.

Research & Innovation

Developing next-generation solutions for the energy transition.

To support our energy transition strategy, Saron Inc. has established a dedicated Research & Innovation team focused on developing and implementing technologies and practices that advance our capabilities in handling new energy products. Our innovation efforts span from incremental improvements to transformative concepts that could reshape the storage industry.

Materials Research

Investigating advanced materials and coatings that can withstand the unique challenges posed by alternative fuels, including hydrogen embrittlement prevention, low-temperature resistance for cryogenic applications, and corrosion protection for biofuels.

Monitoring Technologies

Developing sophisticated monitoring systems that provide real-time data on product quality, environmental conditions, and system integrity. These technologies include advanced sensors, machine learning algorithms for predictive maintenance, and digital twins of terminal operations.

Energy Efficiency

Creating innovative approaches to reduce energy consumption and carbon emissions in terminal operations, including waste heat recovery, smart energy management systems, and electrification of equipment previously powered by fossil fuels.
